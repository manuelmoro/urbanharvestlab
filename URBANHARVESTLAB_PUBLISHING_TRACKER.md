## Notes for Writers

- Write with the publication order in mind. Every article should link only to pages that already exist at the time it is drafted for publication, unless the linked page is scheduled in the same publishing batch and confirmed to ship first.
- For orders 1-5, publish the core system pages first and then retrofit cross-links once the hub and later support pages are live. Do not hold foundational articles waiting for the hub.
- Cornerstone articles should connect downward into scenario, care, and diagnosis pages. Supporting articles should link back to their parent cornerstone in the first third of the article, then laterally to 2-3 closely related support pages where useful.
- Troubleshooting pages must link back to root-cause guides such as light, containers, watering, potting mix, or care routine. Do not let symptom pages become isolated dead ends.
- Buyer guides must always link to at least one explainer and one beginner setup page so the commercial pages stay context-rich and editorially credible.
- Once the hub page is live, update earlier cornerstone pages to add a visible link back to the hub. As more supporting content is published, keep expanding the hub so it aggregates the cluster by setup path, light conditions, container decisions, care routine, and common problems.
